# Finite-gap and algebro-geometric methods

This path is for readers who want to understand finite-gap solutions, Riemann surfaces, theta functions, spectral curves, and algebro-geometric constructions in integrable systems.

## 0. Prerequisite repair

Finite-gap methods require a different background profile from standard IST on the line.

Repair topics:

- compact Riemann surfaces;
- divisors and Abel maps;
- holomorphic and meromorphic differentials;
- theta functions;
- spectral curves and branch points;
- basic algebraic geometry language.

Peter Olver's broad applied-mathematics resources and Charles Weibel's algebraic background pages may be useful as side references, but the finite-gap path needs topic-specific sources rather than general algebraic topology.

## 1. Conceptual entry

Start with survey-style or introductory explanations before reading monographs.

Useful seeds:

- Dubrovin, *Theta functions and non-linear equations*.
- Introductory notes on Riemann surfaces and theta functions, to be added after verification.
- Miller resources where finite-gap or modulation methods appear.

## 2. Core reference shelf

Use these as references rather than cover-to-cover first readings.

- Belokolos et al., *Algebro-geometric approach to nonlinear integrable equations*.
- Gesztesy--Holden, *Soliton Equations and their Algebro-Geometric Solutions*.
- Krichever--Shiota for the Riemann-Schottky problem and advanced algebraic-geometric connections.

## 3. Bridge to RHP and computation

Finite-gap material becomes especially useful for this guide when it connects to RHP, numerical computation, or asymptotic regions.

Important bridge seed:

- Trogdon--Deconinck, *A Riemann-Hilbert problem for the finite-genus solutions of the KdV equation and its numerical solution*.

## 4. What to avoid initially

Do not start by collecting every finite-gap paper. Keep the path focused on training resources, core references, and papers that explain reusable constructions.
