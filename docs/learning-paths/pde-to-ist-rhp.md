# From PDE background to IST/RHP

This path is for a reader who knows graduate analysis and some PDE, but wants to enter inverse scattering, Riemann-Hilbert methods, and long-time asymptotics for integrable PDE.

## 0. Background repair

Use these only where needed.

- Sobolev spaces, Fourier analysis, and harmonic analysis: Tao's analysis/PDE notes, Monica Visan's dispersive PDE notes, Laugesen's harmonic analysis notes, and KIT Fourier Analysis if the course materials are accessible.
- Functional analysis and spectral theory: Michael Taylor notes, KIT Functional Analysis, and standard spectral-theory references.
- Complex analysis and contour methods: Tao complex-analysis course material, Peter Olver complex-analysis notes, and RHP-specific notes once basic complex analysis is stable.

## 1. First IST pass

Start with expository inverse-scattering resources before attempting a long asymptotic paper.

Recommended order:

1. Peter Perry, *Inverse Scattering in 60 Minutes*.
2. Peter Perry lecture page, especially the 2025 inverse-scattering lecture PDFs.
3. Peter Perry, *Inverse scattering and global well-posedness in one and two space dimensions*.
4. Peter D. Miller Math 651 Winter 2018 lectures on KdV, NLS, direct scattering, time evolution of scattering data, and RHP formulation.

Goal: understand the direct scattering map, the inverse problem, the evolution of scattering data, and why the inverse problem naturally becomes a Riemann-Hilbert problem.

## 2. RHP formulation

After the first IST pass, move to RHP structure.

Recommended resources:

- Peter D. Miller Math 651 lectures on the RHP of inverse scattering.
- Percy Deift, *Riemann-Hilbert Problems*.
- Tom Trogdon homepage and RHP masterclass notebooks for concrete computational models.

Goal: understand jump matrices, normalization, pole conditions, Beals-Coifman formulations, small-norm RHPs, and uniqueness/solvability mechanisms.

## 3. Nonlinear steepest descent

Read this stage with a specific model equation in mind.

Recommended resources:

- Miller Math 651 lectures on defocusing NLS long-time asymptotics.
- Deift's RHP lecture notes.
- Olver--Trogdon, *Nonlinear steepest descent and the numerical solution of Riemann-Hilbert problems*.
- The Deift--Zhou mKdV paper as a historical research-paper target, not as the first source.

Goal: understand phase functions, signature tables, lens opening, model RHPs, parametrices, and error RHP estimates.

## 4. Research-paper transition

Once the above is comfortable, choose one paper cluster rather than a broad literature sweep.

Possible clusters:

- defocusing NLS long-time asymptotics;
- DNLS with weighted Sobolev data;
- dbar steepest descent;
- soliton resolution through RHP;
- finite-density or finite-gap backgrounds.

At this stage, the guide should point to bibliography signals but should not pretend that every research paper is a learning resource.
