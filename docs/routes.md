# Research routes

A route is not a formal syllabus. It is a short reading order for entering a research problem. Each route separates prerequisite repair, core training, paper preparation, and reference material.

## Route A: PDE background to IST/RHP

Use this route if the reader has analysis or PDE background but has not yet learned inverse scattering or Riemann-Hilbert methods.

| Stage | Goal | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| Prerequisite repair | Make the PDE and spectral language usable. | Sobolev spaces, Fourier analysis, linear dispersive estimates, basic spectral theory. |
| Core training | Learn how a Lax pair leads to scattering data and inverse reconstruction. | Introductory IST notes, NLS/KdV examples, scattering maps, simple RHP formulations. |
| Paper preparation | Move from examples to the method used in current papers. | Model RHPs, jump factorizations, contour deformation, long-time asymptotics. |
| Reference shelf | Keep reliable references nearby. | Monographs, survey notes, and carefully reviewed course pages. |

Typical model equations: NLS, DNLS, KdV, mKdV, AKNS-type systems.

## Route B: RHP to nonlinear steepest descent

Use this route if the reader already knows the basic RHP formulation and wants to understand asymptotic analysis.

| Stage | Goal | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| Prerequisite repair | Stabilize complex-analysis and asymptotic-analysis tools. | Cauchy transforms, Plemelj formulas, stationary phase, steepest descent. |
| Core training | Understand the Deift-Zhou method at the level of examples. | Scalar conjugation, lens opening, model problems, local parametrices. |
| Paper preparation | Learn how the method is adapted to concrete equations. | Defocusing/focusing NLS, soliton sectors, oscillatory RHPs, dbar extensions. |
| Reference shelf | Compare analytic and numerical viewpoints. | RHP lecture notes, numerical RHP papers, software examples. |

## Route C: Finite-gap and algebro-geometric methods

Use this route if the target problem involves Riemann surfaces, theta functions, periodic/quasi-periodic solutions, or finite-gap asymptotics.

| Stage | Goal | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| Prerequisite repair | Build the geometric background. | Compact Riemann surfaces, divisors, Abel maps, theta functions. |
| Core training | Learn how spectral curves encode integrable solutions. | Finite-gap construction for KdV, NLS, or related hierarchies. |
| Paper preparation | Connect the classical construction to modern asymptotic or numerical work. | Modulation, finite-genus RHPs, theta-function parametrices. |
| Reference shelf | Keep classical and modern references separate. | Classical AG monographs, finite-gap papers, numerical finite-genus examples. |

## How to extend a route

Add a resource only when it has a clear job in the route. A link should answer at least one of these questions: what background does it repair, what method does it teach, what paper-reading obstacle does it remove, or what technical reference role does it serve?
