# Core topics

This page is a compact topic map. It explains how the main mathematical areas relate to each other, without turning each topic into a separate mini-article.

## Spectral and inverse-scattering methods

The entry point is the passage from a nonlinear evolution equation to an auxiliary linear spectral problem. The main objects are Lax pairs, scattering data, direct and inverse scattering maps, discrete spectrum, reflection coefficients, and reconstruction formulae.

This topic connects most directly to Route A.

## Riemann-Hilbert problems and asymptotics

RHP methods recast inverse problems as analytic factorization problems. For long-time behavior, the central mechanism is nonlinear steepest descent: transform the RHP until the leading asymptotic behavior is captured by model problems and controlled error estimates.

This topic connects most directly to Route B.

## Model equations and nonlinear waves

The guide treats equations such as NLS, DNLS, KdV, mKdV, and AKNS-type systems as model problems rather than as separate silos. An equation should appear here because it teaches a method, not merely because it is integrable.

Related PDE-side topics include dispersive estimates, soliton stability, modulation, orbital/asymptotic stability, and soliton resolution.

## Finite-gap and algebro-geometric methods

Finite-gap methods use spectral curves, divisors, Abel maps, theta functions, and Baker-Akhiezer functions to construct exact or asymptotic solutions. They are closely related to periodic and quasi-periodic solutions, modulation theory, and finite-genus RHP constructions.

This topic connects most directly to Route C.

## Numerical and reproducible methods

Numerical RHP, spectral computations, finite-gap numerics, and reproducible nonlinear-wave experiments should be included when they help readers test or visualize a method. Software links belong in the resource table, while reproducible group outputs belong under Group work.
