# Curation policy

This guide should remain selective. A resource is worth adding only if it advances research training in a specific way.

## Preferred resource types

Highest priority:

- researcher homepages with lecture notes, course pages, slides, or reading lists;
- university course pages with substantial mathematical material;
- seminar, workshop, program, and summer-school pages with preserved notes, slides, or videos;
- public lecture notes and open book drafts;
- key surveys, textbooks, and software resources when they are directly useful for research training.

Lower priority:

- isolated PDF files without author, date, or institutional context;
- generic mathematical resource lists;
- introductory popular expositions;
- pages with no recoverable teaching or research material.

## Metadata discipline

Every mature entry should record title, author, institution, resource type, URL, topic tags, equation tags, method tags, prerequisites, level, recommended use, strengths, limitations, quality grade, link status, and last checked date.

## Review rule

Unverified entries may remain in `data/seed-candidates.yml`, but they should not be promoted to curated topic pages until they have been checked manually.
