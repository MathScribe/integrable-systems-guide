# Inverse scattering transform

This topic page is for direct and inverse scattering methods for integrable evolution equations.

## Scope

Core subjects include Lax pairs, Jost solutions, scattering coefficients, reflection coefficients, discrete spectrum, norming constants, Marchenko equations, trace formulae, conservation laws, and reconstruction of solutions from scattering data.

Important model equations include KdV, mKdV, defocusing and focusing NLS, DNLS, sine-Gordon, Toda-type models, and AKNS systems.

## Direct starting links

Start with resources that expose actual lecture notes rather than only bibliographic references.

1. [Peter Perry lectures page](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~perry/lectures.htm). The 2025 Tsinghua lecture PDFs are the cleanest quick entry in this guide.
2. [Perry Lecture 1: Lax Representations and What to Do With Them](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~perry/Tsinghua-2025/Tsinghua-Lecture-1.pdf). Use this for the first pass through Lax pairs and scattering data.
3. [Perry Lecture 2: Large-Time Asymptotics for the Defocussing NLS Equation](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~perry/Tsinghua-2025/Tsinghua-Lecture-2.pdf). Use this when moving from IST to nonlinear steepest descent.
4. [Peter D. Miller Math 651, Winter 2018](https://websites.umich.edu/~millerpd/CurrentCourses/651_Winter18.html). Use this as the systematic course page: KdV, Toda, NLS, RHP formulation, long-time asymptotics, and dbar steepest descent.
5. [Peter Perry, *Inverse scattering and global well-posedness in one and two space dimensions*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.01009). Use this after the lecture PDFs for a more self-contained written treatment.
6. [KIT IST seminar, Summer 2023](https://www.math.kit.edu/iana2/edu/semist_liao2023s/). Keep this as a high-priority candidate until the notes and links are manually checked.

## What a good resource should provide

A strong IST resource should explain both the spectral problem and the time evolution of scattering data. It should also make clear which function spaces are assumed, which boundary conditions are used, and how solitons appear as discrete spectral data.

## Reading checkpoints

After a first IST pass, the reader should be able to answer:

- What is the spectral problem associated to the PDE?
- What are the scattering data?
- How does time evolution act on the scattering data?
- Where do solitons enter as discrete spectral data?
- How does the inverse problem become a Riemann-Hilbert problem?
- Which parts depend on decay, smoothness, or weighted Sobolev assumptions?
