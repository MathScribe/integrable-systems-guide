# Finite-gap and algebraic-geometric methods

This page is for finite-gap integration, spectral curves, Riemann surfaces, theta functions, Baker-Akhiezer functions, and algebro-geometric solutions of integrable systems.

## Scope

Core subjects include hyperelliptic curves, Abelian differentials, Abel maps, theta functions, Baker-Akhiezer functions, divisor dynamics, finite-gap potentials, Whitham modulation, and algebro-geometric solutions of KdV, NLS, mKdV, and related systems.

## Direct starting links

1. [Dubrovin, *Theta functions and non-linear equations*](https://www.mathnet.ru/eng/rm2891). Classical conceptual reference for theta functions in integrable systems.
2. [Belokolos et al., *Algebro-geometric approach to nonlinear integrable equations*](http://digitool.hbz-nrw.de:1801/webclient/DeliveryManager?pid=2184180&custom_att_2=simple_viewer). Major finite-gap reference; verify stable access.
3. [Gesztesy--Holden, *Soliton Equations and their Algebro-Geometric Solutions*](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/soliton-equations-and-their-algebrogeometric-solutions/0239D84F9561EB2BFA452BAAE4B0B686). Reference shelf for algebro-geometric solutions.
4. [Holden companion page](https://holden.folk.ntnu.no/solitons/index1.php). Possible companion material for Gesztesy--Holden; needs verification.
5. [Krichever--Shiota, *Soliton equations and the Riemann-Schottky problem*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1111.0164). Advanced algebraic-geometric signal, not a first resource.
6. [Trogdon--Deconinck, finite-genus KdV RHP article](https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0167278913000444). Useful bridge between finite-gap solutions and numerical RHP.
7. [Oliver's course notes repository](https://codeberg.org/killlakill/courseNotes). Candidate source for local notes on algebro-geometric solutions and Riemann-surface computations; link only stable public notes once reviewed.

## Role in research training

Finite-gap methods are a bridge between integrable PDE, algebraic geometry, asymptotic analysis, and the theory of Riemann surfaces. Resources here should be selected carefully because notation and prerequisites vary widely.

## Reading checkpoints

- Can you describe the spectral curve and its branch points?
- Which Abelian differentials are normalized, and on which cycles?
- How are the Abel map and theta function used to reconstruct the solution?
- What is the divisor data?
- Is the resource using finite-gap, Whitham, RHP, or numerical language?
