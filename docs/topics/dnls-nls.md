# DNLS and NLS

This page is for nonlinear Schrödinger equations and derivative nonlinear Schrödinger equations, especially where PDE, spectral, IST, and RHP methods intersect.

## Scope

Core subjects include focusing and defocusing NLS, derivative NLS, Kaup-Newell systems, Chen-Lee-Liu and Gerdjikov-Ivanov forms, gauge transformations, AKNS systems, weighted Sobolev scattering, pure soliton solutions, modulational instability, rogue waves, and long-time asymptotics.

1. [Miller Math 651, Winter 2018](https://websites.umich.edu/~millerpd/CurrentCourses/651_Winter18.html). Use the NLS lectures for direct scattering, time evolution, RHP reconstruction, long-time asymptotics, focusing NLS, and solitons.
2. [Perry Lecture 2: Defocusing NLS long-time asymptotics](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~perry/Tsinghua-2025/Tsinghua-Lecture-2.pdf). Use as a compact NLS asymptotics entry.
3. [Perry, *Inverse scattering and global well-posedness in one and two space dimensions*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.01009). Use for defocusing cubic NLS and Davey-Stewartson II.
4. [Monica Visan expository notes](https://www.math.ucla.edu/~visan/lecturenotes.html). Use for PDE-side NLS and nonlinear dispersive background.
5. [Trogdon RHP masterclass materials](https://faculty.washington.edu/trogdon/). Use the defocusing NLS notebook after the RHP formulation is familiar.
6. [Dmitry Pelinovsky homepage](https://pelinovsky.mcmaster.ca/). Use as a seed for DNLS/NLS, stability, and nonlinear-wave references; still needs content-level review.

## Why this page is separate

DNLS and NLS deserve a separate topic page because they sit at the intersection of several methods: harmonic-analysis PDE, integrable spectral theory, RHP methods, finite-gap theory, and asymptotic soliton resolution.

## What a good resource should provide

A useful resource should state which version of NLS or DNLS it treats, which spectral problem is used, whether the equation is focusing or defocusing, and what boundary or decay assumptions are imposed.

## DNLS-specific checklist

DNLS entries should be tagged more carefully than generic NLS resources. Record:

- which DNLS form is used: Kaup-Newell, Chen-Lee-Liu, Gerdjikov-Ivanov, or gauge-equivalent form;
- whether the spectral problem is normalized at infinity, at zero, or both;
- whether the scattering theory assumes Schwartz, weighted Sobolev, or rougher data;
- whether the paper treats pure solitons, radiation, soliton resolution, or transition regions;
- whether the result is PDE well-posedness, IST bijectivity, RHP asymptotics, or finite-gap/rogue-wave construction.
