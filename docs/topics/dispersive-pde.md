# Nonlinear dispersive PDE

This page is for the PDE approach to nonlinear dispersive equations, including well-posedness, scattering, concentration compactness, stability, blow-up, and asymptotic dynamics.

## Scope

Core subjects include Fourier restriction and Strichartz estimates, local and global well-posedness, conservation laws, Morawetz estimates, profile decompositions, concentration compactness, rigidity arguments, scattering, blow-up, and stability of coherent structures.

## Relation to integrable systems

Many integrable equations are also central examples in nonlinear dispersive PDE. The PDE viewpoint often supplies function-space estimates, compactness arguments, and stability frameworks that complement IST/RHP methods.

## Candidate seed resources

The first verification pass should include Monica Visan, Terence Tao, Dmitry Pelinovsky, Todd Kapitula, Mathew Johnson, Michael Taylor, KIT Fourier and functional analysis courses, and relevant workshop pages from Fields Institute and similar programs.
