# Nonlinear dispersive PDE

This page is for the PDE approach to nonlinear dispersive equations, including well-posedness, scattering, concentration compactness, stability, blow-up, and asymptotic dynamics.

## Scope

Core subjects include Fourier restriction and Strichartz estimates, local and global well-posedness, conservation laws, Morawetz estimates, profile decompositions, concentration compactness, rigidity arguments, scattering, blow-up, and stability of coherent structures.

## Direct starting links

3. [Tao, 245C Notes 4: Sobolev spaces](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/2009/04/30/245c-notes-4-sobolev-spaces/). Use as a quick Sobolev-space reference.

5. [KIT Fourier Analysis and applications to PDE](https://www.math.kit.edu/iana2/edu/fourieranal2021w/). Candidate prerequisite course; needs manual checking.
6. [KIT Dispersive Equations](https://www.math.kit.edu/iana2/edu/dispequ2022s/). Candidate PDE-side course; needs manual checking.
7. [Linares--Ponce, *Introduction to Nonlinear Dispersive Equations*](https://link.springer.com/10.1007/978-1-4939-2181-2). Standard textbook reference.

## Relation to integrable systems

Many integrable equations are also central examples in nonlinear dispersive PDE. The PDE viewpoint often supplies function-space estimates, compactness arguments, and stability frameworks that complement IST/RHP methods.

## Reading checkpoints

- Which estimates control the nonlinear term?
- What is the solution space and what topology is used?
- Which conservation laws are used?
- Does the result prove local well-posedness, global well-posedness, scattering, blow-up, or asymptotic stability?
- Does the argument use compactness or an explicit transform such as IST?
