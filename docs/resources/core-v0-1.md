# Core resources v0.1

This page records the first small batch of manually verified core resources. The structured metadata lives in `data/resources.yml`.

## S-grade starting points

### Peter D. Miller homepage

Use this as a stable entry point for integrable systems, nonlinear waves, special functions, random matrix theory, and asymptotic methods. It is especially useful because it connects research interests, teaching history, presentations, textbook corrections, and course-related material.

Primary URL: <https://websites.umich.edu/~millerpd/>

### Peter D. Miller teaching page

Use this page to identify Miller's course history and auxiliary teaching materials. The teaching history explicitly includes Math 557 Asymptotic Analysis and past topics courses on integrable systems and Riemann-Hilbert problems.

Primary URL: <https://websites.umich.edu/~millerpd/Teaching.html>

### Peter A. Perry, *Inverse scattering and global well-posedness in one and two space dimensions*

Use this as a bridge between inverse scattering and PDE well-posedness. It is particularly relevant for defocusing cubic NLS in one dimension and defocusing Davey-Stewartson II in two dimensions.

Primary URL: <https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.01009>

### Percy Deift, *Riemann-Hilbert Problems*

Use this as a compact entry into nonlinear steepest descent for Riemann-Hilbert problems and its applications to integrable systems, special functions, orthogonal polynomials, and random matrices.

Primary URL: <https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.08304>

## A-grade complementary resources

### Monica Visan homepage and expository notes

Use these for the nonlinear dispersive PDE side of the guide, especially NLS, critical regularity, scattering, and related dispersive equations. These resources are not primarily IST/RHP resources, but they are important PDE-side complements.

Primary URLs:

- <https://www.math.ucla.edu/~visan/>
- <https://www.math.ucla.edu/~visan/lecturenotes.html>

### Sheehan Olver and Thomas Trogdon, *Nonlinear steepest descent and the numerical solution of Riemann-Hilbert problems*

Use this for the bridge between Deift-Zhou deformation ideas and numerical RHP methods. It is especially useful once the reader has basic RHP vocabulary.

Primary URL: <https://arxiv.org/abs/1205.5604>
