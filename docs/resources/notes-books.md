# Notes and books

This page will collect lecture notes, open notes, book drafts, surveys, and textbook entry points.

## Inclusion criteria

A notes or book entry should be included only if it is mathematically useful for research training. Preference is given to resources that contain precise statements, proofs, examples, problem sets, or a strong bibliography.

## Initial seed targets

- AMS Open Math Notes entries relevant to integrable systems, PDE, spectral theory, Fourier analysis, and Riemann surfaces
- Michael Taylor notes for analysis and PDE background
- Peter Olver notes and book materials relevant to symmetry, applied mathematics, and integrable systems
- Charles Weibel notes for algebraic or topological background where relevant
- Selected book drafts or surveys on inverse scattering, RHP methods, nonlinear steepest descent, and finite-gap integration

Bare PDF links should be avoided unless the origin, author, version, and topic role are clear.
